{{<alert "circle-info" >}}
**Summary**: 
This blog will introduce a convenient extension named 'Color Highlight' to automatically display RGB colors in Visual Studio Code.
{{< /alert >}}

First, open the 'Visual Studio Code', then search 'Color Highlight' in the ‘extensions’, and install it.

{{<figure src="/blog/en/20230829vscodeautorgb/20230829vscodeautorgb-1.png" caption="Figure 1" width="400">}}

Second, click the "gear-like button" and click the "Extension Settings".

{{<figure src="/blog/en/20230829vscodeautorgb/20230829vscodeautorgb-2.png" caption="Figure 2" width="400">}}

Third, check the option inside the red box, showed in Figure 3. 

{{<figure src="/blog/en/20230829vscodeautorgb/20230829vscodeautorgb-3.png" caption="Figure 3" width="400">}}

Finally, you can enjoy your RGB color during writing in Visual Studio Code!

{{<figure src="/blog/en/20230829vscodeautorgb/20230829vscodeautorgb-4.png" caption="Figure 4" width="400">}}
